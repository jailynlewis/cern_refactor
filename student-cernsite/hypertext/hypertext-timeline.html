Short History of Hypertext

1945
Vannevar Bush proposes "Memex", a conceptual machine to store and link information.
1965
Ted Nelson coins the word "Hypertext".
1967
Andy van Dam and others build the Hypertext Editing System and FRESS at Brown University.
1968
Doug Engelbart demonstrates the NLS system ("Mother of All Demos").
1975
ZOG (now KMS) is developed at Carnegie Mellon University.
1978
Aspen Movie Map, the first hypermedia videodisc, MIT.
1984
Filevision from Telos: hypermedia database for Macintosh.
1985
Symbolics Document Examiner, Janet Walker; InterMedia, Brown University, N. Meyrowitz.
1986
OWL introduces "Guide", the first widely available hypertext system for PCs.
1987
Apple introduces HyperCard; Hypertext '87 Workshop.
1990
ECHT (European Conference on HyperText) expands the field.
